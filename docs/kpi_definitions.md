KPI Definitions — Mini Institutional Retention BI Environment

Overview

This document defines the core KPIs used within the First-Year Retention and Student Success dashboards.
All definitions are standardised to ensure consistency across dashboards and analytical layers.

The primary analytical scope is:

Year 1 Entrants (First-Year Students)

All KPIs operate at the grain of:

Student – Academic Year

1. Institutional Retention (Year 1 → Year 2)

Definition
Percentage of Year 1 entrants who re-enrol in the following academic year anywhere within the institution.

Numerator
Distinct Year 1 entrants with retained_institution_next_year_flag = 1.

Denominator
Distinct Year 1 entrants (entrant_flag = 1).

Purpose
Measures institutional-level first-year continuation performance.

2. Same-Programme Retention

Definition
Percentage of Year 1 entrants who progress to Year 2 within the same programme.

Purpose
Evaluates programme-level continuity and within-programme progression.

3. Retained vs Not Retained (Count)

Definition
Absolute count of Year 1 entrants retained or not retained into Year 2.

Purpose
Provides volume context alongside percentage measures.

4. High-Risk Students (Count)

Definition
Number of Year 1 students classified as High Risk based on Success Score segmentation.

Purpose
Quantifies the scale of students potentially requiring academic support intervention.

5. High-Risk (% of Cohort)

Definition
Proportion of Year 1 students classified as High Risk.

Purpose
Provides risk concentration indicator for strategic monitoring.

6. Retention Rate — High Risk

Definition
Retention rate among students classified as High Risk.

Purpose
Validates effectiveness and predictive strength of risk segmentation model.

7. Average Success Score

Definition
Mean composite success index across the Year 1 cohort.

Purpose
Provides cohort-level academic engagement and performance summary metric.
